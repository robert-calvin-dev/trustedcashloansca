What Is a Payday Loan?

A payday loan is a short-term, high-interest loan designed to help you cover urgent costs between paychecks. Loan amounts typically range from $100 to $1,000 depending on your state and eligibility. Repayment is generally due on your next payday, or within 7–31 days.

Where Are Payday Loans Available?

Payday loans are available in many U.S. states, but not all. Approval is based on your residency and your state’s lending regulations. If your state does not allow payday loans, you may still be eligible for a Personal Installment Loan.

APR Disclosures & Terms

The Annual Percentage Rate (APR) for payday loans varies by state, lender, and individual qualifications. It may range from 124% to 700% depending on the loan amount and repayment schedule. All lenders in our network are expected to disclose exact terms before you agree to any loan.

Example Cost Breakdown

Borrowing $500 in Texas with an APR of 460% may result in a total repayment of $1,100 over 31 days. Always review terms carefully.

Responsible Borrowing

Payday loans are designed for emergency use only and are not long-term financial solutions. Borrow only what you can repay and avoid rolling over or extending loans, as this may result in additional fees.
