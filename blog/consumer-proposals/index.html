What Is a Consumer Proposal — and Does It Exist in the U.S.?

Published by Trusted Cash Loans • May 19, 2025

If you’ve been researching debt relief, you may have come across the term “consumer proposal.” It’s a legal option in Canada — but what exactly is it, and is there a U.S. equivalent? Let’s break it down so you understand what your real options are if you’re drowning in debt but want to avoid bankruptcy.

What Is a Consumer Proposal?

A consumer proposal is a formal agreement between you and your creditors, filed through a Licensed Insolvency Trustee in Canada. You offer to repay a portion of what you owe over time, based on your ability to pay — and if your creditors accept, it becomes legally binding. It’s an alternative to bankruptcy, and once filed, creditors must stop collection efforts and wage garnishments.

Does the U.S. Have Consumer Proposals?

No — consumer proposals are a Canadian legal process. But there are similar debt relief options available to Americans, including:

  • Chapter 13 Bankruptcy: A court-supervised repayment plan over 3 to 5 years. It protects your assets and allows structured repayment.
  • Debt Management Plans (DMPs): Offered by credit counseling agencies. You make a single monthly payment to the agency, which pays your creditors. Interest may be reduced.
  • Debt Settlement: Negotiating lump-sum payoffs for less than you owe. Risky and often involves fees, but used by some people as a last resort.

When Is a Structured Repayment Plan a Good Idea?

If you:

  • Have steady income but can’t keep up with minimum payments
  • Want to avoid bankruptcy but need relief from mounting interest
  • Are dealing with wage garnishments or aggressive collections
— then a repayment plan through bankruptcy or a credit counselor may help you reset without fully starting over.

How Does This Compare to a Loan?

A debt relief plan reduces or restructures what you already owe. A loan, on the other hand, gives you new money — often to consolidate or replace your current debts. If you have decent income and limited credit damage, a personal installment loan can:

  • Replace multiple debts with one fixed payment
  • Lower your total interest (depending on your rate)
  • Keep your credit report cleaner than bankruptcy or settlement

Can I Get a Loan If I’m in a Repayment Plan?

Possibly — but it’s more difficult. Many lenders won’t approve new credit while you’re in Chapter 13 or on a DMP. However, some short-term lenders or alternative credit products (like payday loans) may still be available if you need emergency funds during this time.
