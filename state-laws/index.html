U.S. State Payday & Installment Loan Laws

Payday and personal loan regulations vary by state. At Trusted Cash Loans, we comply with all local lending laws and only connect borrowers to lenders authorized to operate in their jurisdiction. Below is a general overview of payday and installment loan legality across the United States.

Payday Loan Availability by State

States Where Payday Loans Are Legal:
Alabama, Alaska, California, Delaware, Florida, Idaho, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Michigan, Mississippi, Missouri, Nevada, North Dakota, Ohio, Oklahoma, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia, Washington, Wisconsin, Wyoming

States with Strict Limitations or Bans:
Arizona, Arkansas, Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Montana, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, Oregon, Pennsylvania, South Dakota, Vermont, West Virginia

If you live in a state where payday lending is restricted, you may still qualify for a personal installment loan with longer repayment terms and more flexible underwriting standards.

Installment Loan Regulations

Installment loans are available in most states, though APR caps, maximum loan amounts, and licensing requirements vary by jurisdiction. Trusted Cash Loans only partners with lenders that follow applicable state and federal laws, including:

  • Truth in Lending Act (TILA)
  • Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
  • Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA)
  • State-specific rate and term restrictions

APR Caps & Disclosures

Some states impose interest rate caps on small-dollar loans. For example:

  • Colorado: 36% APR cap
  • Connecticut: 12% APR cap
  • New York & New Jersey: Payday loans are illegal regardless of APR
Our lending partners are required to disclose all APRs, repayment schedules, and total repayment amounts before you accept any offer.

Borrower Protections

All lenders in our network are required to:

  • Clearly display all loan terms prior to agreement
  • Honor state-mandated cancellation periods (where applicable)
  • Provide fair and non-deceptive practices under federal law
  • Allow early repayment without penalty

Using Loans Responsibly

State laws are designed to protect borrowers from debt cycles, excessive interest, and hidden fees. We strongly advise using short-term loans only for urgent, unavoidable expenses, and reviewing all terms carefully before accepting funds.

Check Your State’s Laws

For the most up-to-date regulations in your state, visit your Attorney General’s office or the official Consumer Protection Bureau in your area. You can also email us at compliance@trustedcashloans.com for help understanding your local lending rules.
